A Glimpse of our Pioneer!

Winchester International English School (WIES) is the realization of a dream of visionary Dr. Afsar Khan, who once passionately believed in imparting high quality English medium Education in his hometown Aurangabad.

Its motto "Aspiring for Excellence" endeavours to provide a learning platform to diverse cultures.
With 32 years of experience of heading various schools since 1985 till 2017 in the Middle East, perseverance and dedicated effort in the field of Education he visualizes (WIES) as a school with a difference wherein the focus is on the all-round growth and development of each individual child whose quest for knowledge unholds only where true learning takes place.

THE INFRASTRUCTURAL UNITS INCLUDED IN WINCHESTER SCHOOL

INFRASTRUCTURE

  • CLASSROOMS

    The classrooms are well equipped with all the essential learning materials, well ventilated and brightly coloured which help the students to focus and take interest in their studies.

  • LABORATORIES

    To support theoretical teaching in Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Maths, Computer Applications, and English language, the school has well-equipped and separate laboratories.

  • LIBRARY

    To inculcate the habit of reading in the young minds, we encourage our students to make the full use of our spacious, airy, well -lit & fully furnished library.

  • COMPUTER LAB

    The school has a state of the art auditorium with a stage, anterooms, an orchestra pit and a seating capacity of 800 students. It is the preferred venue to conduct events on special occasions.

  • PLAYGROUND

    A fleet of buses is maintained by the school to provide transport facilities to students and teachers to commute to and fro. The buses are managed by caring and trained staff.

  • Transportation

    Keeping in mind the habit of healthy eating, our school provides lunch in the campus as per a healthy eating plan. The school has in-house cooks who prepare hygienic and tasty meals.

Meet Our Team

The foundation of every great team is a direction that energizes, orients, and engages its members. Teams cannot be inspired if they don’t know what they’re working toward and don’t have explicit goals.

Those goals should be challenging (modest ones don’t motivate) but not so difficult that the team becomes dispirited. They also must be consequential: People have to care about achieving a goal, whether because they stand to gain extrinsic rewards, like recognition, pay, and promotions; or intrinsic rewards, such as satisfaction and a sense of meaning.
This kind of communication is much easier to learn in a school environment then on your first project in a new job. And it can save you lots of frustration, or even costly mistakes, later on. Teamwork can also improve the outcome of a school project because you’re able to draw upon each other’s unique strengths. This ability, of seeking the expertise and ability of other people will serve you well in your academic pursuits—and throughout your career
